Michael Getachew Tadesse

water resources engineering · coastal engineering · statistical modeling · machine learning ·

Michael joined the CoRE Lab in Fall 2018. He is currently a PhD student at UCF (Civil Engineering PhD). He has a background in Civil Engineering from Addis Ababa University (Ethiopia) and holds a master’s in Water Science and Engineering (Erasmus Mundus Master’s in Flood Risk Management). Shortly before joining UCF, he has been a research intern at the Danish Hydraulic Institute (DHI). Currently, he is working on a NASA funded project that focuses on the use of data-driven models to simulate the past, present, and future storm surges and their associated impacts. Some of his research interests include the application of statistical, machine learning and deep learning models on water resource engineering problems and the coupling of data-driven models with numerical models.

research

Modeling Global Storm Surges in the Past, Present, and Future and the Associated Socio-Economic Impacts

Data-Driven Modeling of Global Storm Surges

This study lays the foundation for investigating the changes in frequency/intensity of extreme sea levels for which SLR is a main driver. It also shows that data-driven models can be used as an alternative to simulate global storm surges, better yet, it calls for the coupling of data-driven and numerical models for a robust storm surge modeling.
